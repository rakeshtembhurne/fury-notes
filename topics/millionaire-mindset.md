# Millionaire Mindset — Consolidated Notes

## The Psychology of Wealth

### Financial Thermostat
- Napoleon Hill wrote "Think and Grow Rich"
- Died bankrupt despite millions of book sales
- Didn't become "rich on the inside"
- 70% of lottery winners go broke
- Haven't altered their wealth thermostat internally
- Source: Paul McKenna

### RAS and Wealth
- Reticular Activating System (RAS) filters beliefs
- If you feel unworthy, finds evidence of unworthiness
- Must reprogram filter through visualization
- Source: Mel Robbins

## Why Hard Work Doesn't Lead to Career Growth

### The Problem
- Working harder at same things doesn't move needle
- Like throwing darts at same spot, missing bullseye
- Nobody cares how hard you work if hitting wrong target
- Only care about value you create

### The Invisible Work Problem
- You're doing visible work, not valuable work
- Refactoring legacy code = feels good, creates no visible value
- Senior engineers plateau while others glide up ladder
- It's not politics, luck, or who you know
- Source: Steve Quinn / A Life Engineered

## Millionaire CEO's 8 Steps

### Step 1: Spend Money to Save Time
- Rich prioritize time over money
- Don't mow lawn, don't get own groceries
- Invest dollars to buy back hours
- Virtual assistants for few dollars/hour globally

### Step 2: Solve Problems for Rich People
- Easier to work with
- Pay on time
- Less drama
- "Broke people are a pain in your butt"

### Step 3: Sell Before You Build
- Find customer FIRST
- All software companies, coaching programs: sell first
- Validates demand before building

### Step 4: Manage Energy, Not Time
- Structure day by energy levels
- High-energy tasks morning
- Low-energy tasks afternoon
- Don't respond to whoever needs your time

### Step 5: Simplicity Scales, Complexity Fails
- Fight for simplicity
- Don't add features
- Complexity is ceiling of growth

### Step 6: Cash Flow is Critical
- Don't finance customers
- Cash flow is oxygen

### Step 7: If Not "Hell Yeah!" It's "No"
- Only do exciting things
- Everything else is noise

### Step 8: Network = Net Worth
- Surround with people who are where you want to be

## The Confidence Paradox

### Speaking Like a Top 1% CEO
- Speak in headlines
- Start with the point, not preamble
- Give headline first, frame conversation
- Make people lean in instead of tune out

### Decisive Speech
- Clear, direct statements
- Limited hedging
- Few qualifiers or apologies
- Signals competence

### The Formula
- "I have a problem that's leading to missed revenue"
- "Can we discuss no longer than 5 minutes"
- "I found a problem, I have a solution, I need 30 seconds to get an okay"
