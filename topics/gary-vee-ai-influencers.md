# Gary Vee: 100 Billion AI Influencers Are Coming

## Key Predictions
- 100 billion FAKE AI influencers coming in 36 months — indistinguishable from real humans
- Small DTC brands should use AI NOW (big brands wait due to backlash)
- Live shopping/social commerce is the future — already $8-10B GMV on Whatnot

## Selfish vs. Selfless Content Framework
| Selfish | Selfless |
|---------|----------|
| Focus on views/likes | Focus on audience value |
| "What can they do for me" | "What can I do for them" |
| Plateau eventual | Compounds over time |

## Platform Mandate (Bare Minimum)
Must post to: TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat Spotlight, YouTube Shorts, YouTube long-form, LinkedIn, Twitter/X

> "If you're not posting the same content to YouTube Shorts, you're not doing the bare minimum to protect yourself from disappearing."

## Niche Strategy
- Go deeper, not broader
- Show the "uncool" parts of yourself (that's the differentiator)
- Running club example: build community around specific niche interest

## The Real Moat (What AI Can't Do)
- IRL events and experiences
- Replying to every comment/DM
- Monthly Q&As
- Real human connection

## The Journalist Strategy
Hire a journalist/writer, not just film people:
1. Listens to all content/interviews
2. Identifies interesting threads
3. Asks 15 detailed follow-up questions
4. Produces long-form first-party written content

## Mental Fortitude
- Track: Average views per post (not virality)
- "You're so addicted to the cheering, you're vulnerable to the booing"
- Detach from positive feedback addiction

## Key Quotes
- "100 billion people that are pretty and charismatic are about to join social media."
- "The more you are selfless, the more you will win."
- "The human thing is about to explode in value."
- "AI can't do that."

### Key Quotes (Extended)
> "100 billion people that are pretty and charismatic are about to join social media. Unlike you, they are not real. But like you, they will seem real. In 36 months, you will not be able to tell the difference."

> "Most people in this room will sit around and hope and pray that AI influencers don't happen. Or they'll say 'AI influencers are bad cuz it's not authentic' while they pedal content they don't even like."

> "It takes me 5 seconds to look at your last 15 pieces of content and when it's 15 out of 15 that it was selfish for you. You wanted to look glamorous. You wanted them to buy something. You posted it cuz someone paid you."

> "There's 8 billion people on Earth, and there's about to be 100 billion fake people joining the market every day. If you're just trading on looking good, you're going to lose."

> "Before posting, ask: 'Why don't I want this to happen?' The answer usually reveals your selfish financial or fame insecurity. That's a bad strategic framework."

> "The world is about to be a barbell. We're going extreme digital. AI is not far away. And I think one of the biggest opportunities for this room that can combat AI influencers is real life."

> "I am obsessed with analog people. AI can't do that."

> "You're so addicted to the cheering, you're vulnerable to the booing."

> "Be more careful of everyone who's saying you're gorgeous and unbelievable and the best and the smartest. That's what's making you vulnerable."

> "I have no relationship with virality. I'll take it when it happens. In fact, I don't celebrate. I try to analyze. When I get virality, I'm not like, 'Yay, I'm cool.' I'm like, 'Fuck, why?'"
