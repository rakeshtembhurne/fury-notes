# Dan Martell: 30 Brutal Business Truths I Wish I Knew in My 20s

## Top 10 Truths

1. **Spend money to save time** — In 2026, you can buy back hours with delivery, apps, VA support. Time is more valuable than money.

2. **Solve problems for rich people, not broke people** — Rich people are easier to work with, pay on time, less drama. Broke people = pain in the butt.

3. **Sell before you build** — Default to finding a customer FIRST. Every software company, coaching program, media venture: sell first, then build.

4. **You don't manage time, you manage energy** — Structure your day by energy levels. High-energy tasks in morning, low-energy in afternoon.

5. **Simplicity scales, complexity fails** — Fight for simplicity. Don't let people keep adding features. Complexity is the ceiling of growth.

6. **Cash flow is critical** — Don't finance your customers. Cash flow is oxygen for a business.

7. **If it's not a "Hell Yeah!" it's a "No"** — Only do things that excite you. Everything else is noise.

8. **The best time to start was 5 years ago. The second best time is today.** — Stop procrastinating.

9. **Your network is your net worth** — Surround yourself with people who are where you want to be.

10. **Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, cash is king** — Focus on profit and cash flow, not just revenue.

## Key Quote
> "Simplicity scales, complexity fails."
