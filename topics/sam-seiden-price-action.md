# Sam Seiden — Supply/Demand & Price Levels

## Core Thesis
**Swing highs and lows mean nothing on their own.** A swing low is just a low price. A swing high is just a high price. They do not tell you why price moved, who was trading there, or whether institutions placed orders there. If institutions did not place orders at a level, that level is worthless. You are trading against professionals with order flow data, trading desks, and deep market structure understanding. They care about one thing: **imbalance.**

---

## The Problem with Traditional Support and Resistance

- A popular video shows someone drawing a line at a swing low, calling it support, price bounces, they make money.
- The trader tries this themselves — draws lines at every swing high and low, chart looks like a "prison," and none of the levels work consistently.
- **The levels that work are the ones where institutions placed orders. The ones that don't are random.**

---

## How Institutions Actually Trade

When a large institution (hedge fund, bank) wants to buy a large position:
1. They **cannot** just hit a buy button for the full amount — there are not enough sellers at the current price. Buying $10M all at once spikes the price and gets terrible fills.
2. They place **limit orders** (buy orders) at specific price levels and wait for price to come down.
3. They accumulate positions slowly over time.
4. Even with limit orders, they cannot fill everything at once. An **imbalance** forms.

**The key insight:** The reason price bounces at certain levels is because of supply/demand imbalance created by institutional order accumulation — not because of arbitrary swing highs or lows.

---

## What Makes a Price Level Work

A price level works when:
- There is a **supply/demand imbalance** at that level
- Institutions placed large orders there
- Price has previously reacted to that imbalance (bounced or reversed)

A price level fails when:
- It is a random swing high or low with no institutional significance
- No order flow justification exists

---

## The Trader's Challenge

- Retail traders use tools (lines, indicators) that institutions don't care about.
- Institutions can see order flow, stop losses, retail positioning.
- They can trigger stop losses (stop hunting) before moving price in the intended direction.
- **The only edge is understanding where real institutional orders are placed — i.e., where supply/demand imbalances exist.**

---

## Key Rules from Sam Seiden

- **Random swing highs and lows have no inherent meaning.** Only levels with institutional order flow significance work.
- **Supply/demand imbalance is the only reason price bounces or reverses.**
- **Institutions accumulate positions at specific levels using limit orders, not market orders.**
- **If you don't know why a level should work, it won't work consistently.**
- **You're trading against professionals. Know what they're looking at: order flow, not your chart patterns.**
