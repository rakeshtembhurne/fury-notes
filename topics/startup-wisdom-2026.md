# Startup Wisdom 2026 — Consolidated Notes

## Core Startup Principles

### The Sell-Before-You-Build Framework
- Default to finding a customer FIRST before building anything
- Every software company, coaching program, media venture: sell first, then build
- This validates demand before wasting time on product development
- Source: Dan Martell

### Boring Businesses Build Billionaires
- Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger, Sam Walton built wealth in unsexy businesses
- Focus on essential products people need — socks, insurance, retail
- Master economies of scale in niche markets
- One small town in China produces 1/3 of world's socks — $8B pairs annually
- Humility allows you to bet on businesses most people ignore
- Source: Michal Mujgos

### The One Thing Framework
- Your SaaS should do ONE specific thing incredibly well
- Don't try to be everything to everyone
- Solve one problem perfectly
- Simplicity scales, complexity fails
- Complexity is the ceiling of growth
- Source: Greg Isenberg

### Mid-Market Sales Strategy
- Sell 1,000 things at $10,000 each (mid-market)
- Easier to find than enterprise, quicker to sell than SMB
- Highest price-for-effort ratio
- "Hunt deer, not rabbits or elephants"
- Source: Dan Martell

## Viral Free Tool Marketing Loop
1. Build a free tool (grader, calculator, analyzer) that gives instant value
2. Results create sharing incentive (comparison, competition)
3. Tool markets your business forever — SEO compounds
4. Upgrade path: free tool leads to paid solution for the problems it identified

## Solo Founder Playbook to $7K MRR
1. Set up alerts for every keyword in your niche ("one person everywhere" illusion)
2. Respond to everything on Twitter, Reddit, and relevant communities
3. Build in public daily — share progress, revenue numbers, learnings
4. Post 1-3x a day with clear formatting
5. Reply to people in your community consistently
6. Repeat until you viral, then double down

## The New Way to Build a Startup (20X Companies)
- Best teams automating ALL internal functions, not just one or two
- Tiny teams beating huge incumbents thanks to internal automation
- Leanness is their superpower
- Automate: code, support, marketing, sales, hiring, QA
- Compound startups: build multiple integrated products in parallel
- Source: Y Combinator

## Deep Work (Cal Newport / MIT PhD Method)
- One skill that makes more money than crypto, stocks, real estate: **Deep Work**
- Top students spent LESS time studying but maximized focus
- Activate "Sage Mode" by training brain to enter deep work state at will
- Key: 30 minutes/day for 30 days of focused work = 900 minutes accumulated
- Science-based approach from theoretical computer scientist
- Source: Alex Dekora / Cal Newport

## The 30 for 30 Protocol
- 30 minutes a day for 30 straight days
- Low intimidation: small daily commitment
- Meaningful commitment: forces you to choose something that matters
- Radical compounding: 900 minutes of accumulated focused effort
- 3 rules that make failing almost impossible
- Source: Sahil Bloom

- At $30/month = $300K/month recurring income
