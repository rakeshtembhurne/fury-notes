# Social Media SEO: Search Everywhere Optimization

## Core Thesis

> "Your content strategy can't just be rank on Google. It has to be discoverable everywhere your buyers are looking."

## The Search Everywhere Reality

| Platform | Daily Searches |
|----------|---------------|
| Google | 13.7 billion |
| Instagram | 6.5 billion |
| Amazon | 3.5 billion |
| TikTok | billions |
| YouTube | billions |
| Apple App Store | 500 million |

**73% of modern "search" happens outside of Google.**

## Platform-Specific Search Psychology

### TikTok
- Decisions driven by **emotions and novelty**
- People don't want to think — they want to feel
- Content needs to be immediate, visual, emotionally resonant

### YouTube
- About **retention and perceived expertise**
- People come to learn and evaluate
- Need depth, authority, and proof

### ChatGPT
- All about **citations and semantic clarity**
- AI wants clear, factual information from authoritative sources
- Flashy visuals don't matter — substance does

### Amazon
- Pure **social proof and trust**
- People scroll to reviews, not product descriptions
- Real experiences from real people

### Instagram
- **Aspirational identity**
- People buy into a lifestyle, a version of themselves
- Not just products — transformation

### Reddit
- **Raw authenticity**
- Any hint of marketing speak gets destroyed
- People want honest, unfiltered opinions

## The Google Trap

> "You're optimizing for visibility in one place while your customers are deciding everywhere else."

### The Constellation of Micro-Decisions
Modern customer journey isn't a funnel — it's a constellation:
- **What to click** → Google
- **What to trust** → Reddit/Reviews
- **What to buy** → Amazon, TikTok Shop
- **What to try** → App Store
- **What to think** → YouTube/Podcasts
- **What to believe** → ChatGPT/Claude
- **Who to follow** → Instagram/LinkedIn
- **Who to cite/reference** → AI pulls from everywhere

## Visibility vs. Validation

> "Visibility is showing up in search results. Validation is being mentioned in the conversation."

| Visibility | Validation |
|------------|------------|
| Having a TikTok account | Someone referencing your brand in their TikTok |
| Ranking on Google | Being cited by ChatGPT when asked for recommendations |
| Showing up in search | Being part of the trust network |

## RICE Framework for Platform Prioritization

| Letter | Factor | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **R** | Reach | How many people search on that platform daily? |
| **I** | Impact | How much business impact could this have? |
| **C** | Confidence | How confident are you that you can succeed here? |
| **E** | Ease | How easy is it for you to execute? |

Score each 1-10, multiply by reach number. For most businesses: **2-3 platforms maximum**, not 10.

## Social Media SEO Tactics

### Discovery Starts in the Feed, Not Search
- Algorithms show solutions before intent forms
- Win inside the feed, not the search bar
- Content must deliver clarity in first few seconds
- Show the outcome first

### How People Search on Social
- Full questions with context, not keyword phrases
- "What's the best project management tool for small teams?"
- "How do I create a budget if I'm inconsistent with income?"

### Content Must Be Structured for Extraction
- Clear headlines and section breaks
- On-screen text reinforcing main points
- Subtitles so platforms can read spoken words
- Natural language matching how people search

### On-Platform Transactions = Ranking Signals
- Instagram boosts posts using native checkout
- TikTok rewards creators generating purchases through TikTok Shop
- YouTube pushing on-platform product tagging

**The difference:**
- "Click link in bio to buy" → algorithm sees exit, reach drops
- "Tap product tag to buy now" → algorithm sees retention, reach increases

## Social Content for AI Discovery

### How AI Systems Use Social Content
- ChatGPT and Perplexity pull from Reddit, YouTube, Quora
- Majority of most cited sources are social
- When someone asks ChatGPT for a tool recommendation, it pulls from Reddit threads and YouTube reviews

### What to Measure
1. **Search traffic on each platform** — Instagram, TikTok, YouTube all show search reach analytics
2. **Engagement signals** — Shares and saves signal lasting value, boost search visibility
3. **Brand mentions and questions** — Track when people mention your brand in comments, Reddit, AI conversations

## Platform Prioritization by Audience

### B2C → Prioritize
- Instagram (6.5B daily searches)
- YouTube (3.5B daily searches)

### B2B → Focus On
- LinkedIn
- YouTube

## Key Tactics

1. **Question-based hooks** — Mirror how people naturally speak
2. **Descriptive captions** — Match what people actually type into search
3. **Use AnswerThePublic** — Find questions people are searching across all platforms
4. **Consistent posting** — Fresh content signals relevance to algorithms
5. **Engage authentically** — Help people genuinely, position as expert naturally
