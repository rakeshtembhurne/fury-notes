## Key Frameworks

### The 6 Story Locks (Addictive Content)

Callaway's framework for creating content that hooks and retains viewers:

1. **Term Branding** — Anchor complex ideas to memorable, repeatable terms
2. **Embedded Truths** — Deliver truth the audience already believes (requires less proof)
3. **Pattern Interruption** — Break expectations at the right moment
4. **Micro-Friction** — Add small tension points that compel continued watching
5. **Paradigm Shifts** — Change how the audience sees something
6. **Paradise** — Show them a better way/place they could be

**Core Principle:** Paradise without friction doesn't exist. You must interrupt patterns to maintain attention.

---

### The 6 Hook Power Words

A template for writing hooks that stop the scroll:

1. **Subject** — Who is this about?
2. **Action** — What should they do or what happens?
3. **Objective** — What will they learn/gain?
4. **Contrast** — Before vs. after, old vs. new
5. **Proof** — Who succeeded? What were the results?
6. **Time** — How fast can they achieve it?

**Formula:** Subject + Action + Objective = Hook that converts

---

### The Dopamine Ladder (6 Levels of Addictive Content)

Content builds engagement through six ascending levels:

| Level | Name | What It Is | Key Action |
|-------|------|------------|------------|
| 1 | Stimulation | Initial hook (visual/auditory) | Open with high-energy editing, quick cuts |
| 2 | Captivation | Hold through curiosity | Pose interesting questions, bold claims |
| 3 | Anticipation | Create curiosity gap | "I'll tell you X, but first..." |
| 4 | Validation | Close the loop | Deliver on every promise made |
| 5 | Likability | Make them like you | Show personality, humor, relatability |
| 6 | Pavlovian Response | Train them to return | Consistent schedule, signature elements |

**Critical Rule:** You cannot skip levels. Every video must earn its way up the ladder.

---

### The 4 Hook Mistakes (Callaway's "Four Horsemen")

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Delay** | Context comes too late | First sentence gives topic clarity in 1-2 seconds |
| **Confusion** | Words unclear | Simplify to 6th grade reading level |
| **Irrelevance** | Viewer unsure it's for them | Use "you/your" not "I/me" |
| **Disinterest** | No curiosity | Create contrast loop (A vs B) |

**Speed to Value:** Topic introduction must happen in the first 1-2 seconds.

## Key Insights

### On Creating Content

- Content is free to post — this has never existed in history
- The algorithm doesn't change you — it **exposes** you
- Posting on 7 platforms daily is more important than knowing how to balance your checkbook
- Mr. Beast spends $150K testing thumbnails for each video — it's about commitment level, not dollar amount
- Don't post based on trends or ego — put strategic thought into craft

### On Story Structure

- The story must be about **them**, not you (no "my dad died... and here's what I learned about B2B")
- Every piece of content needs ONE core message delivered multiple ways
- The hook delivers the topic clarity + curiosity; the body delivers the value; the close delivers the transformation
- Pattern interrupts must be strategically placed, not random

### On Video/Short-Form

- The first 3 seconds determine everything — hook with topic clarity immediately
- Contrast creates curiosity: the distance between what viewers believe and your alternative
- Speed to value is critical — every second of delay causes exponential drop-off
- For YouTube specifically: packaging (title/thumbnail) is 90%, content is 10%

### On Measurement

- Track cost per **closed deal**, not cost per lead
- Viral views ≠ brand value — focus on monetizable content format
- Most people post based on trends or ego, with zero strategic thought
