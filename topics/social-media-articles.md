## Key Frameworks

### Game Theory Framework (Callaway)

The complete playbook: **Players → Incentives → Games → Strategies**

#### The 7 Players
| # | Player | Description |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | You | The content creator |
| 2 | The Algorithm | Platform's recommendation engine |
| 3 | Passive Viewers | Interested but not potential buyers |
| 4 | Active Viewers | Have pain points, potential buyers |
| 5 | Entertainment Creators | Competitors going for mass views (CPM/brand deals) |
| 6 | Education Creators | Competitors driving conversions (bottom-of-funnel) |
| 7 | Brands & Advertisers | Paying creators for sponsored content |

#### The Two Games (Pick ONE per channel)
| Dimension | Awareness Game | Conversion Game |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Goal | Max views at all costs | Max on-target conversions |
| Monetization | CPMs + brand deals | Products/services |
| Audience | Passive (entertainment) | Active (buyers) |
| Content | Emotional + curiosity | Tactical + practical |
| TAM | Broad (everyone) | Narrow (buyers only) |

#### The 4 Horsemen of Each Game

**Awareness (Entertainment):**
1. Sharability — Be first or different enough to earn social credit
2. Curiosity Hook — Contrast against conventional belief
3. Emotional Payoff — Evoke emotion by the end
4. Large TAM — Simple topics lots of people understand

**Conversion (Education):**
1. On-Target Sharability — Share to peers with same problems
2. On-Target TAM — Only topics valuable to buyers
3. Problem/Solution Clarity — Impossible to misunderstand
4. Tactical Solve — Non-obvious, immediately implementable

**The Walkaway Value Test:** Can the viewer walk away and make a change that improves their life or business? Yes = education. No = entertainment in disguise.

### The 4 Social Media Strategies for Business Owners

| Strategy | Brand Type | Content Type | Best For |
|----------|------------|--------------|----------|
| **1** | Personal Brand | Entertainment | Individual experts, creators, coaches |
| **2** | Personal Brand | Education | Consultants, course creators, pro services |
| **3** | Traditional Brand | Entertainment | E-commerce, consumer brands, products |
| **4** | Traditional Brand | Education | B2B, SaaS, service companies |

**The Decision Matrix:**
- Are you the product? → Personal brand
- Is your product the product? → Traditional brand
- Want to go viral? → Entertainment
- Want to convert? → Education

### The 6 Hook Power Words

A template for writing hooks that stop the scroll:

1. **Subject** — Who is this about?
2. **Action** — What should they do or what happens?
3. **Objective** — What will they learn/gain?
4. **Contrast** — Before vs. after, old vs. new
5. **Proof** — Who succeeded? What were the results?
6. **Time** — How fast can they achieve it?

### LinkedIn Personal Brand Lead Machine (Lara Costa)

**The SLAY Framework:**
| Letter | Element | Purpose | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| **S** | Story | Gets attention | "I read many books but this one changed my life" |
| **L** | Lesson | Gets retention | "Here are the 6 principles from that book" |
| **A** | Actionable Advice | Gets conversion | Step-by-step breakdown to implement today |
| **Y** | You | Gets engagement | End with question or close pointed at reader |

**The 4-3-2-1 Content System:**
- 4 posts per week across 3 content pillars: Growth, TAM, Sales
- 20-30 minutes per post using the system
- First 30 minutes after posting are critical — respond to every comment

**Authority Jacking:** Photos with well-known figures create halo effect and algorithmic distribution.

### Meta "Ugly Ads" Framework (2025)

**The Thesis:** Creative and messaging are the ONLY things that matter. Campaign parameters have been absorbed by AI.

**Why "Ugly" Works:**
- Meta admitted "Perfection Fatigue" — Millennials and Gen-Z want lo-fi mobile content
- Ugly ads look like organic Facebook group posts → pattern-match as content, not ads
- Raw, low-production static images outperform polished creatives by 2x

**The 2-Step Framework:**
1. Write your natural message (how you'd tell a friend)
2. Design the "rough draft" — then STOP. That IS your actual creative.

### AI Influencer Content (Aftab's Framework)

**Why Dancing AI Videos Don't Get Brand Deals:**
- No trust, identity, or repeatability
- Brands want: Trust/Identity, Repeatability, Content Match

**The Solution: AI Character with Personality**
- Create a character with distinct personality, POV, and consistent identity
- Workflow: Pinterest → Leonardo → Hicksfield
- Focus on identity + consistency over viral content

## The Interest Media Era (Gary Vaynerchuk)

**The Core Thesis:** We have moved from social media to interest media. Content is judged on merit, not follower count.

| Old Model (Social Graph) | New Model (Interest Graph) |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| Build followers, they see your posts | Content shown based on interest, not who you follow |
| Follower count = reach | Content quality = reach |
| Like email marketing | Merit-based discovery engine |

**The Goldilocks Zone (2026-2030):**
- AI not good enough to replace humans, but easy enough to use
- Best time to grow — will never be this easy again
- Personal brand critical — build now before AI takes over
- Human trust premium will increase as AI content rises

**The 7-Platform Strategy:** Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, Snapchat — post on all daily.

**Gary's Predictions:**
- Google search is where Yellow Pages were in 1999 (~24 months before serious disruption)
- AI is doing to Google what Google did to Yellow Pages
- The only moat left in a decade is BRAND
- In ~24 months, prompt a video and AI generates it
- AI tools will be commodity; knowing how to use them is the differentiator

## Platform-Specific Strategies

### LinkedIn (B2B/Personal Brand)

- Generates 80% of all B2B leads
- Decision-makers guaranteed to see your content
- Competition is mostly AI slop — your bare minimum is their highest quality
- Trends from Twitter are still fresh on LinkedIn (7-day arbitrage)
- Start organic first; boost what works with ads
- Thought leadership ads + video + simple images work best

### Amazon Streaming Ads (Neil Patel)

- Amazon now reaches 80% of streaming households (Netflix, Spotify, Roku)
- Amazon's unfair advantage: knows what you actually BUY
- This is the 2005-2010 moment for streaming ads
- First-party purchase data — doesn't need cookies
- Inventory still underpriced — early movers build the playbook

### Reddit Marketing

- 88% of Reddit users made a purchase based on info found on Reddit
- Reddit users don't hate buying — they hate being sold to
- Reddit shows up in Google search, AI Overviews, and ChatGPT answers
- Attribution looks broken because conversions happen later on Google, not Reddit
- Reddit ads can be as cheap as $3 CPMs
- Organic strategy: 3 phases (Crawl → Walk → Run over 4+ months)

### Facebook/Meta Ads (Post-Andromeda)

- Campaign parameters fully absorbed by AI — only creative and messaging remain
- Go broad — let algorithm optimize
- 20-60 creatives minimum needed
- Split CBO by AOV — Meta will spend on lowest AOV products
- Test new creatives every 3-7 days
- Best performing: UGC Video, Aesthetics Call Out, Outfit Style, Founder Story

## Key Metrics That Matter

| Metric | What It Measures | How to Improve |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Average View Duration (AVD)** | How long viewers watch | Speed to value, pattern interrupts |
| **Shares** | Algorithm satisfaction | Create shareworthy content (be first/different) |
| **Platform Bounce Rate** | Did they leave platform? | End with engagement prompt |
| **Cost Per Closed Deal** | True acquisition cost | Not cost per lead — track the full cycle |
| **Watchtime Session Share** | % of someone's session you capture | Build series content |
