# Trading Journal — Compiled Lessons

## Recurring Psychological Patterns

### The Hope Trap
You have a thesis. The market invalidates it. Instead of accepting the invalidation, you hold onto the old thesis and wait. And wait.

**Fix:** If the analysis says BUY and market is BUYING — accept it. Don't wait for fiction.

### The Revenge Trade Spiral
1. Took a wrong position size → loss
2. Tried to recover by over-trading in the wrong direction
3. Each loss made the next trade more desperate
4. Discipline completely gone

**Fix:** After any loss, mandatory 15 min break. If you feel the urge to "go bigger" — that's ego, not trading.

### The Gap Trap
After a gap, patience breaks. You feel like you've "missed" the move. So you force a trade in the wrong direction.

**Fix:** Gap open = wait for first candle confirmation. No trades for 30 min after gap open.

### Trading Against Your Own Analysis
When you know the correct direction but your brain wants to trade the opposite, you're not trading — you're gambling.

**Fix:** If you catch your brain searching for trades in the WRONG direction — STOP. Step away.

## The Rules

### On Analysis
- If the setup is clear and you can't act on it — DO NOTHING. Waiting is not missing.
- If the setup is bullish and you're looking for shorts — you're already wrong. Close the chart.
- Believe your analysis. If it says UP and price is UP, don't fight it.

### On Position Size
- Position size is non-negotiable. ONE bad size trade can wipe out several good ones.
- Position size is set BEFORE the trade — not during.
- Default to smaller when you're emotional.

### On After Losses
- After a losing trade, the worst thing you can do is trade AGAIN to "make it back."
- After a wrong-direction trade, mandatory 15 min break.

### On Trading Frequency
- Max 3 trades per day — if you hit 3, you're done regardless of P&L.
- No trades for 30 min after gap open.

> Hope is not a strategy. When your analysis says UP but you're hoping for DOWN — you're no longer trading. You're guessing.
