# Trading Journal — 2026-03-23

## Market Context
Market gap opened today. The setup was clearly negative — direction of sellers.

## What Happened
I knew the setup was bearish. Should have been selling. But I couldn't wait. My brain started searching for reasons to buy — finding trades in the opposite direction of what I actually knew was correct.

## Psychology Pattern Observed

**Mistake 1 — Wrong Direction:**
- Knew: Should sell (bearish setup after gap)
- Did: Tried to find buy setups
- Result: Fighting against own analysis

**Mistake 2 — Oversized Position + Over-Trading:**
- Knew: Position size should be controlled
- Did: Took a position too high
- Then: Over-traded in wrong direction
- Result: Losses stacked on losses

**What was happening mentally:**
- Impatience after the gap move
- Brain rationalizing entries in wrong direction
- Position size discipline broke under pressure
- Trying to "make back" losses by taking more trades
- Each wrong trade made the next one more desperate

## The Core Problem

> When you know the correct direction but your brain wants to trade the opposite, you're not trading — you're gambling. Your brain is looking for permission to act on impulse, not on analysis.

**The gap trap:** After a gap, patience breaks. You feel like you've "missed" the move. So instead of waiting for the next valid setup, you force a trade in the wrong direction just to feel like you're "in the game."

**The revenge trade spiral:**
1. Took wrong position size → loss
2. Tried to recover by over-trading in wrong direction
3. Each loss made the next trade more desperate
4. Discipline completely gone

## Rules to Remember

**Rule #1:** If the setup is clear and you can't act on it — DO NOTHING. Waiting is not missing. Forcing a trade because you're bored or impatient is how you lose money.

**Rule #2:** When you catch your brain searching for trades in the WRONG direction — that's your signal to STOP. Step away. You're not in the right headspace to trade.

**Rule #3:** Position size is non-negotiable. ONE bad size trade can wipe out several good ones. If you feel the urge to "go bigger" — that's ego, not trading.

**Rule #4:** After a losing trade, the worst thing you can do is trade AGAIN to "make it back." That's revenge trading. Take a break first.
