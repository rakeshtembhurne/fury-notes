# The Almanack of Naval Ravikant

> [!abstract] The Central Thesis
> Both wealth and happiness are learnable skills—not outcomes of luck or external circumstances. Wealth comes from specific knowledge + accountability + leverage. Happiness comes from desire removal and present awareness. The internet has democratized both, but most people haven't figured this out yet.

> [!quote] The Bookmark Quote
> "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep. Money is how we transfer time and wealth. Status is your place in the social hierarchy."

---

## Core Formula

| Concept | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Specific Knowledge** | Build unique skills through genuine curiosity |
| **Accountability** | Take public risks under your name |
| **Permissionless Leverage** | Use code and media to scale |
| **Desire Removal** | Reduce wanting = increase happiness |
| **Long-term Games** | Partner with patient, high-integrity people |

---

## Part 1: Wealth

### Chapter 1: Understand How Wealth Is Created

**Key Insight:** You won't get rich renting your time—you must own equity in a business.

- Wealth = assets earning while you sleep
- Money = transfer mechanism
- Status = zero-sum hierarchy game
- **Action:** Define what wealth means to you—specific assets, not income
- **System:** Stop trading time for money; seek equity

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Working harder at the wrong thing won't make you rich. Direction matters more than effort.

---

### Chapter 2: Find and Build Specific Knowledge

**Key Insight:** Specific knowledge cannot be taught—it must be discovered through obsession and curiosity.

- If society can train you, it can train someone else and replace you
- Building it feels like play to you but looks like work to others
- **Question:** What would you do even if you weren't paid? That's your specific knowledge.
- **System:** Build expertise through practice, not formal education

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Following trends instead of passion leads to replaceable skills.

---

### Chapter 3: Play Long-Term Games with Long-Term People

**Key Insight:** Pick partners with integrity—skills can be learned, character cannot.

- All returns in life—wealth, relationships, knowledge—come from compound interest
- Don't partner with cynics and pessimists—their beliefs are self-fulfilling
- **Framework:**
  - Choose partners with: High intelligence, High energy, Above all—Integrity
- **System:** Build relationships with patient, like-minded people

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Partnering with cynics creates self-fulfilling prophecies of failure.

---

### Chapter 4: Take on Accountability

**Key Insight:** Taking public risk builds reputation and attracts opportunities.

- Embrace accountability and take business risks under your own name
- Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage
- Your name is your greatest asset
- **Action:** Take one public stand or risk this month
- **System:** Build personal brand alongside business

---

### Chapter 5: Find a Position of Leverage

**Key Insight:** Code and media are permissionless leverage available to everyone.

- Fortunes require leverage
- **Types of Leverage:**
  - Code (permissionless)
  - Media (permissionless)
  - Capital (permissioned)
  - Labor (permissioned)
- **Action:** Learn to code—even basics give leverage
- **System:** Create content that scales without you

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Chasing permissioned leverage limits accessibility.

---

### Chapter 6: Get Paid for Your Judgment

**Key Insight:** Better judgment = compound returns.

- Judgment—specifically applied specific knowledge—gets paid
- You're not paid for your time, you're paid for your judgment
- Time has linear returns; judgment has exponential returns
- **Action:** Develop strong opinions, weakly held
- **System:** Track your judgments and learn from outcomes

---

### Chapter 7: Prioritize and Focus

**Key Insight:** Direction > Effort. Figure out WHAT before grinding HOW.

- The most important thing: figure out what you should work on
- Don't grind until you figure it out
- Focus on one thing until mastery
- **Action:** Spend time figuring out your direction before working hard
- **System:** Regular strategy reviews prevent drift

---

### Chapter 8: Build or Buy Equity in a Business

**Key Insight:** You will get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get. At scale.

- To gain financial freedom, you must own equity—not just rent your time
- You can build equity (start a company) or buy equity (invest)
- **Evaluation:** Build, buy, or invest?

---

### Chapter 9: Judgment

**Key Insight:** Decision-making is everything—better decisions compound.

- Judgment—the ability to make good decisions—is the most important skill
- Comes from specific knowledge applied with experience
- Rationality can be trained
- **System:** Build a toolkit of mental models
- **Study:** Decision-making explicitly

---

### Chapter 10: How to Think Clearly

**Key Insight:** Identity creates blind spots—shed it to see clearly.

- To think clearly, you must first shed your identity
- The more your sense of self is tied to outcomes, the less clearly you can think
- Objective observation requires psychological distance
- **Action:** Question your identities regularly
- **System:** Seek evidence against your beliefs

---

## Part 2: Happiness

### Chapter 11: Happiness Is Learned

**Key Insight:** Happiness is what's there when you remove the sense that something is missing.

- Naval went from 2-4/10 to 9/10 over a decade—money helped, but it's a small piece
- Happiness is a skill, like fitness or nutrition
- Every positive thought holds a negative (Tao Te Ching duality)

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Chasing positive thoughts creates negative thoughts—you can't sustain happiness by seeking it.

---

### Chapter 12: Happiness Is a Choice

**Key Insight:** You control your interpretation—nothing external determines your happiness.

- Reality is neutral
- You interpret reality—you have that choice
- Two people in same situation can interpret differently
- **Action:** When upset, question your interpretation
- **System:** Practice seeing situations neutrally

---

### Chapter 13: Happiness Requires Presence

**Key Insight:** The mind exists in motion toward future or past—present stops the motion.

- Happiness is mainly not suffering—not desiring, not thinking too much about the future or past
- It's embracing the present moment and reality as it is
- **Action:** Practice returning to present when mind wanders
- **System:** Mindfulness meditation builds present-muscle

---

### Chapter 14: Happiness Requires Peace

**Key Insight:** Judgments create suffering—peace comes from accepting.

- If you ever want peace in your life, you have to move beyond good and evil
- There are no external forces affecting your emotions
- **Action:** Reduce judgments of good/bad in daily life
- **System:** Practice equanimity

---

### Chapter 15: Every Desire Is a Chosen Unhappiness

**Key Insight:** Desire creates suffering—it's the mind's job to want.

- Every desire you have chooses a unhappiness for you
- The desire is the gap between your current state and what you want
- When you remove the desire, you're back to baseline—happy
- **Action:** Notice when desires cause suffering
- **System:** Consciously choose which desires to pursue

---

### Chapter 16: Envy Is the Enemy of Happiness

**Key Insight:** You only see highlight reels of others.

- Comparing yourself to others creates suffering
- The comparison is never fair because you don't see their full context
- Social media amplifies envy
- **Action:** Unfollow accounts that trigger envy
- **System:** Compare yourself only to your past self

---

### Chapter 17: Happiness Is Built by Habits

**Key Insight:** Happiness is a practice, not a destination.

- Naval's happiness habits:
  - Lower identity
  - Calm mind
  - Don't care about things that don't matter
  - Avoid politics
  - Avoid unhappy people
  - Value time
  - Read philosophy
  - Meditate
  - Hang with happy people
- **Action:** Start with 10-minute daily meditation
- **System:** Build happiness habits systematically

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Waiting to be happy until conditions are perfect = never happy.

---

### Chapter 18: The Meanings of Life

**Key Insight:** You were dead for billions of years before birth, will be after. Use finite time wisely.

- Three answers to "what is the meaning of life?"
  1. It's personal—find your own
  2. There is no meaning—create your own
  3. The meaning is to accelerate toward the heat death of the Universe
- The real truths cannot be spoken—only discovered
- **Action:** Sit with big questions for years, not days
- **System:** Build your philosophy through experience

---

### Chapter 19: Learn to Love to Read

**Key Insight:** Reading is the highest-leverage habit.

- Naval reads 1-2 hours/day—puts him in top .00001%
- That alone accounts for his success
- Making reading a habit is the most important thing
- How you read matters less than reading itself
- **Action:** Read 30 minutes today. Start small.
- **System:** Make reading non-negotiable

> [!warning] Common Failure Mode
> Waiting for "the right book" = never reading.

---

### Chapter 20: Collect Mental Models

**Key Insight:** Multiple models beat single perspective.

- Best mental models come from: evolution, game theory, Charlie Munger, Nassim Taleb, Benjamin Franklin
- Load your head with different mental models for different situations
- Use first principles, not analogies
- **Action:** Study one mental model per week
- **System:** Build a mental model toolbox
