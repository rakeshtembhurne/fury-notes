# Vision

To be a free man—externally liberated by wealth and internally liberated by wisdom.

## MISSION

To play the game of life with 100% intensity but 0% attachment; creating immense value through specific knowledge while remaining a clear vessel for truth, unburdened by identity or status.

## GUIDING MANTRA

> **"Play the game with 100% intensity, but zero attachment. Build from peace, not from hunger."**

To live this out, you have to treat your day like a high-performance engine that is cooled by deep stillness. If you are 100% intense but have no peace, you'll burn out. If you are 100% peaceful but have no intensity, you'll never build your leverage.

## 1. The Morning: The "Internal Game" (Zero Attachment)

Naval says, "All of man's troubles come from his inability to sit quietly in a room by himself."

- The Hour of Silence: Spend the first 60 minutes of your day without a phone. Meditate, walk, or sit. This is where you practice being "nobody." You are anchoring your peace _before_ the marketplace tries to take it.
- Audit Your Hunger: Briefly look at your to-do list. Ask: "Am I doing this because I'm hungry for status, or because it's the right action?" Shift your mindset from _needing_ the result to _loving_ the craft.

## 2. The Deep Work: The "High Intensity" (The Lion)

Once the internal game is set, you switch to the "Worldly Game."

- The 4-Hour Sprint: Dedicate 4 hours to your Specific Knowledge (coding, sales, strategy). During this time, you are a "Lion." No distractions, no multi-tasking.
- Total Presence: Treat the work as a Mantra. If you are writing an email, be the best email writer in the world for those 5 minutes. This is where "100% Intensity" lives.

## 3. The Mid-Day: The "Circuit Breaker"

In the heat of entrepreneurship, the ego starts to tighten.

- Equanimity Checks: Set a timer every 3 hours. Take 3 deep breaths and ask: "If I lost this deal right now, would I still be okay?" If the answer is no, you've slipped into "Hunger." Reset and return to "Peace."
- Right Speech: In every meeting, practice absolute truth. No "salesy" exaggerations. Naval and Buddha both agree: the truth is the most efficient way to run a business because it requires the least amount of mental "processing power."

## 4. The Evening: The "Self-Liquidation"

As the sun goes down, you must "die" to the day's identity.

- Release the Result: Review what happened. Did you win? Did you lose? It doesn't matter. Give the results back to the universe. You did the work; the outcome is no longer your business.
- Intellectual Play: Read books on fundamentals—science, philosophy, or math. This keeps your mind sharp (Naval) while reminding you of the vastness of reality (Buddha).

## 5. The Sleep of the Just

Go to bed with a "zeroed" mind. No lingering unfulfilled desires for tomorrow.

- Morning: Be a Monk (Find Peace).
- Day: Be a Lion (Apply Intensity).
- Evening: Be a Ghost (Let it all go).
