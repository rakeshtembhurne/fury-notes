# Trading Journal — 2026-03-25

## Market Context
Market opened with a directional bias. The setup was clearly bullish — direction of buyers.

## What Happened
I knew the market was going up. The setup was unambiguously bullish. But I was waiting and hoping for it to go down instead. I had a price level in mind where I expected a pullback — and I kept waiting for that pullback that never came.

The market didn't fall. It went up. And I still kept hoping it would go down. I planned and executed a short trade in the wrong direction despite knowing better.

## Psychology Pattern Observed

**Mistake 1 — Trading Against Your Own Analysis:**
- Knew: Market was going up, setup was bullish
- Did: Hoped for a pullback that never came
- Still did: Planned and executed a short
- Result: Fighting the trend, losing money

**Mistake 2 — Hope and Denial:**
- Expected price to go down at a specific level
- Reality: Price never reached that level
- Still held onto the bearish thesis long after it was invalidated
- Result: Persistent denial of the obvious

**Mistake 3 — Over-Traded and Oversized:**
- Took more trades than I should have
- Position size was larger than my rules allow
- Each additional trade compounded the losses
- Result: Significant loss for the day

## The Core Problem

> Hope is not a strategy. When your analysis says UP but you're hoping for DOWN so you can "get a better entry" — you're no longer trading. You're guessing. And guessing while hoping is the most expensive combination in trading.

**The hope trap:** You have a thesis. The market invalidates it. But instead of accepting the invalidation, you hold onto the old thesis and wait. And wait. And then when the market finally moves, you trade in the wrong direction because you've "waited so long."

**The revenge trade spiral:**
1. Market goes up instead of down → loss of opportunity
2. Kept over-planning shorts in a bullish market
3. Over-traded trying to make back what I lost
4. Size got bigger as desperation grew
5. End of day: significant loss

## Rules to Remember

**Rule #1:** If the setup is bullish and you're looking for shorts — you're already wrong. Close the chart. Come back tomorrow. Don't trade against a clear trend because of a price level you "hoped" for.

**Rule #2:** Hope is the enemy of discipline. When you catch yourself thinking "it has to come down here" — ask yourself: what does my analysis actually say? If the analysis says up, and price is going up, believe your analysis.

**Rule #3:** If you miss an entry, you miss it. Do NOT chase the missed move by trading in the opposite direction out of frustration. That's just ego trying to prove you "knew" it would come back.

**Rule #4:** Position size is set BEFORE the trade. When you're emotional, your size will always be wrong. Default to smaller when you're frustrated.
